🦎 How to Play Chameleon

🎯 Game Overview

Chameleon is a social deduction game where one player is secretly the "Chameleon" who doesn't know the secret word. The Chameleon must blend in with the other players while trying to figure out what the secret word is, while everyone else tries to identify who the Chameleon is!

👥 Setup & Players

  • Players: 3-8 players work best
  • Join a Room: Enter your name and either join an existing room with a code or create a new one
  • Wait for Players: The host can start the game once everyone has joined

🎮 How to Play

Phase 1: Giving Clues

  • Each player takes turns giving a one-word clue related to the secret word
  • Regular players should give clues that relate to the secret word but aren't too obvious
  • The Chameleon must give a clue that sounds related without knowing the actual word
  • All clues are displayed for everyone to see

Example Clue Round:

Can you spot which clue might be from the Chameleon?

Phase 2: Voting

  • After everyone gives their clue, players vote for who they think is the Chameleon
  • You cannot vote for yourself
  • The player with the most votes is suspected to be the Chameleon

Phase 3: The Reveal

  • If the Chameleon receives the most votes, they get one chance to guess the secret word
  • If they guess correctly, the Chameleon wins!
  • If they guess wrong, the regular players win
  • If someone else gets the most votes, the Chameleon wins by avoiding detection

🏆 Winning Conditions

🦎 Chameleon Wins If:

• They avoid getting the most votes

• They get caught but guess the secret word correctly

👥 Regular Players Win If:

• They correctly identify the Chameleon

• The Chameleon fails to guess the secret word

💡 Strategy Tips

For Regular Players:

Give Smart Clues

Make your clues specific enough that other regular players know you have the word, but not so obvious that the Chameleon can easily guess it.

Watch for Vague Clues

Look for clues that could apply to many words in the category - these might come from the Chameleon.

Build on Others

Reference previous clues to show you understand the connections, making it harder for the Chameleon to follow.

For the Chameleon:

Listen Carefully

Pay attention to all clues given before your turn. Look for patterns and common themes.

Stay General

Give clues that could reasonably apply to multiple things in the category. Avoid being too specific.

Act Natural

Don't hesitate too much or act suspiciously. Confidence can help you blend in.

⚠️ Important Rules

• Clues must be exactly one word

• No gestures, sounds, or hints outside of your word

• You cannot say any part of the secret word

• No proper nouns (like brand names or specific people)
